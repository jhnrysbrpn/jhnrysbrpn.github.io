" Psych Connect : Empowering Psychology Students as Empathetic Advocates "

"PsychConnect" is a collective initiative led by 1st-year BS Psychology students at Western Mindanao State University in the College of Liberal Arts. Its primary goal is to foster empathy and promote mental health awareness within the College of Liberal Arts community.

Problems Prompting the Establishment of PsychConnect Advocacy in Response to Mental Health Issues within the College of Liberal Arts Community:

  1. Lack of Mental Health Awareness and Stigma:

    Problem: There might be a prevailing lack of awareness about mental health issues and stigma surrounding discussions related to mental health within the College of Liberal Arts community.

    Indicators:

    • Limited discussions or open dialogue about mental health topics among students and faculty.

    • Existence of misconceptions, stereotypes, or negative attitudes towards individuals facing mental health challenges.

    • Reluctance among students to seek help or support due to fear of judgment or stigma.

  2. Insufficient Support Networks and Resources:

    Problem: There might be a lack of adequate support networks or accessible resources available for individuals facing mental health issues within the College of Liberal Arts community.

    Indicators:

    • Limited availability of peer support systems or structured networks for students to seek guidance or share experiences.

    • Students feeling isolated or unsupported in dealing with mental health concerns due to a lack of available resources or platforms

    • While the WMSU Psychology Society operates within the College of Liberal Arts, their initiatives mainly target the Psychology Department exclusively.

  3. Empathy Deficit and Need for Supportive Community:

    Problem: There could be a lack of emphasis on empathy-building and creating a supportive community for individuals experiencing mental health challenges within the College of Liberal Arts setting.

    Indicators:

    • Minimal focus on fostering empathy or teaching empathetic communication skills among students and faculty.

    • Absence of structured platforms or initiatives promoting empathy and understanding among peers regarding mental health issues.

    • Students feeling disconnected or unsupported due to a lack of empathetic interactions or understanding within the university community.

These identified problems have served as catalysts for initiating the PsychConnect advocacy, aiming to address these issues by fostering empathy, raising mental health awareness, and establishing support networks within the College of Liberal Arts community.
